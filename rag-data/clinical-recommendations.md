# Clinical Recommendations (Sample Seed)

## Suspected Heart Failure
- Evaluate dyspnea pattern, orthopnea, edema, fatigue, and exercise tolerance.
- Check vital signs, oxygen saturation, and signs of fluid overload.
- Consider BNP/NT-proBNP, ECG, chest imaging, and echocardiography.
- Initial management often includes diuretics when congestion is present.

## Suspected Influenza
- Typical findings include acute fever, myalgia, malaise, cough, and sore throat.
- Assess high-risk groups (older age, chronic disease, pregnancy, immunosuppression).
- Encourage hydration and symptomatic care; use antivirals in eligible high-risk patients.

## General Differential Guidance
- Match timeline, symptom clusters, and red flags before final diagnosis.
- Use negative findings to narrow the differential rather than ignoring them.
- Reassess diagnosis when treatment response or new data conflicts with initial assumptions.

## Pediatric Fever Note
- In children, age-specific risk stratification is required.
